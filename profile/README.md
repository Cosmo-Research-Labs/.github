## Overview

Cosmo Research Labs (CRL) is an independent research institution focused on foundational engineering and scientific research across aerospace systems and artificial intelligence.

CRL operates on a single principle:

> *The future belongs to the institutions that solve the underlying science.*

The lab works at the level of deep systems engineering, reliability research, autonomous architectures, embedded systems, propulsion research, and machine intelligence — not as isolated products, but as long-term scientific infrastructure. CRL is not building applications. CRL is building the knowledge that applications will depend on.

---

## Philosophy

Modern technology is increasingly built on layers nobody fully understands. CRL exists to work beneath those layers — beneath interfaces, beneath products, beneath trends — at the level where architecture, mathematics, and physics determine what becomes possible.

The objective is not rapid iteration for attention. The objective is durable research.

Every system built here exists to answer a measurable engineering question. Every paper produced here is a permanent contribution to the field. Every dataset released here is infrastructure for the next researcher.

## Featured Research

### Cosmo I&P — Heterogeneous Distributed Avionics Platform

Cosmo I&P (Industrial & Production) is CRL's first major experimental apparatus — a purpose-built research platform for investigating three underresearched failure modes in industrial UAV systems. It is not a flight controller and not a product. It is a controlled experimental substrate designed so that every subsystem generates measurable, publishable data.

**Active research threads:**

**Thread A** — Fault-tolerant state handover in heterogeneous UAV compute stacks. Characterizing AI-to-FMU recovery latency distributions across fault types and flight phases. Target venue: IEEE RA-L / IROS.

**Thread B** — Magnetometer-free yaw estimation via dual-GNSS moving baseline. Validating magnetic-field-independent heading in real industrial EMI environments. Target venue: IEEE Sensors Journal / ION GNSS+.

**Thread C** — Reliable autonomous delivery under progressive sensor degradation. Characterizing graceful fallback hierarchies and minimum viable sensor configurations. Target venue: Journal of Field Robotics.

**Thread D** — Real-time propulsion efficiency optimization via CAN-FD ESC telemetry. Enabling measurable efficiency gains through bidirectional propulsion diagnostics. Target venue: IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics.

Full research proposal available in the `cosmo-ip` repository.

## Research Model

CRL is being built publicly from the ground up. This organization documents experimental systems, failed prototypes, firmware development, simulation environments, research notes, datasets, hardware iterations, and publication work — in full, without filtering for optics.

Science that cannot be reproduced is not science. Every hardware design, firmware module, and experimental dataset produced here will be released openly upon publication.

## Open Collaboration

CRL is actively seeking collaborators for the Cosmo I&P research program. The following roles are open:

**Faculty Supervisor / Academic Advisor** — Expertise in autonomous systems, embedded systems, GNSS/navigation, robotics, or control theory. Primary role is academic oversight and paper review. Research is well-scoped and self-directed; involvement can be light-touch if preferred.

**Co-Author A — Hardware / Embedded Systems** — PCB design (KiCad or Altium), STM32 firmware, CAN-FD, FOC motor control. Contribution to Thread A and Thread D.

**Co-Author B — GNSS / Navigation** — RTK-GNSS systems, EKF-based state estimation, sensor fusion. Contribution to Thread B.

**Co-Author C — Robotics / ROS2** — ROS2, SLAM, perception pipelines, mission planning. Contribution to Thread C.

**Industry Partner** — Access to a real industrial site (factory floor, power corridor, or high-EMI environment) for Thread B validation. Site partner receives full evaluation data under a standard research data-sharing agreement.

To discuss collaboration, reach out at **research@cosmoreslabs.com** with the subject line `CRL Collaboration — [Role]`.
